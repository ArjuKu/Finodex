## The Physical vs. Paper

In commodities, there are **two worlds**:

| World | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Physical** | The actual gold bar, barrel of oil, or bushel of wheat. You can touch it. | A warehouse in Houston with 1 million barrels of oil. |
| **Paper** | The futures contract or derivative. A legal agreement to buy/sell the physical at a future date. | Buying 1,000 barrels of crude oil futures on the CME. |

> **💡 Trader's Note:** As a broker or trader, you live in the "Paper" world, but you must always understand the "Physical" reality. If the physical inventory is tight, the paper price will skyrocket.

## The Commodity Chain

Commodities flow through a physical chain. Understanding this is critical for operations and trading:

### Upstream (Production)
- **What:** Extracting or growing the raw material.
- **Who:** Miners (Gold/Copper), Farmers (Wheat/Coffee), Oil Companies (Crude).
- **Key Metric:** Production cost (AISC for mining, lifting cost for oil).

### Midstream (Transport & Storage)
- **What:** Moving the commodity from the source to the consumer.
- **Who:** Pipelines, Tankers, Railroads, Storage terminals.
- **Key Metric:** Transport cost, Storage cost, Throughput capacity.

### Downstream (Processing & Consumption)
- **What:** Refining the raw material into a usable product.
- **Who:** Refineries (Oil → Gasoline), Smelters (Gold → Jewelry), Food processors.
- **Key Metric:** Refining margins, processing yield.

> **💡 Operations Note:** A disruption anywhere in the chain affects the price. If a pipeline bursts, the "Physical" can't get to the "Downstream," and the price spikes—even if nothing is wrong with the "Paper" market.

## The Players: Producers, Consumers, and Speculators

| Player | Goal | Risk | Example |
|--------|------|------|---------|
| **Producer** | Lock in a price to sell. | If price drops, they lose money. | A gold miner selling futures to hedge. |
| **Consumer** | Lock in a price to buy. | If price rises, they overpay. | An airline buying fuel futures to hedge. |
| **Speculator** | Profit from price movement. | Can lose 100% of capital. | A hedge fund buying oil "on margin." |
| **Broker/Dealer** | Facilitate the trade and earn commission. | Market risk, Counterparty risk. | The physical dealer matching buyer/seller. |

> **💡 Broker's Note:** Your job as a broker is to be the "matchmaker" between the Hedger (who wants protection) and the Speculator (who wants risk). You don't take the risk yourself—you facilitate the trade.

## Types of Commodities

### Hard Commodities (Extracted/Mined)

| Commodity | Unit | Major Markets | Primary Use |
|----------|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Gold** | Ounces (oz) | COMEX, LBMA | Jewelry, Electronics, Currency hedge |
| **Silver** | Ounces (oz) | COMEX, LBMA | Industrial, Jewelry |
| **Crude Oil (WTI/Brent)** | Barrels (bbl) | NYMEX, ICE | Fuel, Plastics |
| **Natural Gas** | MMBtu | NYMEX | Heating, Electricity |
| **Copper** | Pounds (lb) | COMEX, LME | Construction, Wiring |
| **Lithium** | Tonnes | Global | Batteries, EVs |

| **Sugar** | Pounds (lb) | ICE | Food |

## Final Recap

🏃 **Executive Summary:**

1. **Physical vs. Paper:** Know the difference between the actual barrel of oil and the futures contract.
2. **The Chain:** Commodities flow from Upstream → Midstream → Downstream.
3. **The Players:** Producers hedge, Consumers hedge, Speculators take risk—you (the broker) facilitate.
4. **Hard vs. Soft:** Hard = Mined (Oil, Gold); Soft = Grown (Wheat, Coffee).

> **💡 The Golden Rule:** In commodities, **location and timing matter as much as price**. A barrel of oil in Cushing, Oklahoma is not the same as a barrel of oil in Rotterdam.
