# Commodities Interview Guide

## Behavioral & Fit Questions

---

## 1. "Why Commodities?"

### What They're Looking For:
- Genuine interest in commodity markets
- Understanding of the products (energy, metals, agriculture)
- Knowledge of market drivers

### Good Answers:
> "I find commodities fascinating because they have real-world supply and demand dynamics. Unlike equities where you're analyzing company-specific factors, commodities are driven by geopolitics, weather, and global economics. I want to be where those forces translate into trading opportunities."

> "I grew up on a farm so I understand agriculture markets. The seasonal dynamics and weather impact fascinate me, and I see the same principles applying across energy and metals."

---

## 2. "Walk Me Through Your View on [Commodity]"

### Framework:
1. **Current price levels** - Where are we?
2. **Supply picture** - What's the inventory/production?
3. **Demand picture** - What's driving consumption?
4. **Catalysts** - What could move the market?
5. **Your view** - Bull/bear with timeframe

### Example - Oil:
> "WTI is around $75/bbl. On supply, US shale is growing but OPEC+ is managing. On demand, China recovery has been slower than expected. The key catalyst is whether OPEC extends cuts through Q2. I'm neutral near-term but bullish longer-term as demand recovers."

---

## 3. "What's Your View on [Macro Topic]?"

Common topics:
- Fed rate decisions
- China economic growth
- Geopolitical tensions
- ESG/energy transition

### Structure:
1. **What's happening?** - Current state
2. **Why it matters** - Link to commodities
3. **Your view** - Direction and rationale

---

## 4. "How Do You Manage Risk?"

### Key Concepts to Mention:
- **Position sizing** - Don't put too much at risk
- **Stop losses** - Limit downside
- **Diversification** - Don't put all eggs in one basket
- **Hedging** - Use derivatives to reduce exposure

### Example:
> "I manage risk through position sizing - typically no more than 2-3% of portfolio on a single trade. I use stops to limit losses to 1% max. I also hedge with options when volatility is elevated, and I diversify across commodities and timeframes."

---

## 5. "What's the Difference Between Contango and Backwardation?"

### Contango:
- Futures prices > spot prices
- Upward sloping curve
- Normal market (most of the time)
- Roll yield is negative for long positions

### Backwardation:
- Futures prices < spot prices  
- Downward sloping curve
- Typically from supply shortages
- Roll yield is positive for long positions

---

## 6. "What Commodities Are You Watching?"

### Tips:
1. **Pick 2-3** you genuinely follow
2. **Know the drivers** - Supply/demand specifics
3. **Have a view** - Even if uncertain

### Good Answer:
> "I'm watching oil closely - the OPEC+ dynamics are fascinating. Also, copper as a barometer for global growth, and natural gas ahead of winter. In oil, I'm watching whether US production keeps growing and if China's demand recovers."

---

## 7. "How Would You Hedge [X] Commodity Exposure?"

### Example - Airline fuel costs:
> "An airline worried about fuel costs could:
> 1. Buy crude oil calls (limit upside on fuel)
> 2. Sell crude oil puts (reduce cost of hedge)
> 3. Use a collar (buy call, sell put)
> 
> The choice depends on their risk tolerance and budget."
