# The Finance Landscape: Investment Banking vs. Equity Research

If you are new to finance, you might notice that both **Investment Bankers** and **Equity Researchers** use the same tools (like DCFs and Multiples). However, they are playing two completely different games.

---

## 1. Investment Banking (IB): The Transaction
**Objective:** Helping a client buy or sell a **whole company**.

**Key Question:** "What is the fair price for this deal today?"

---

## 2. Equity Research (ER): The Investment
**Objective:** Helping an investor buy or sell **pieces of a company** (shares).

**Key Question:** "Is this stock a Buy, Hold, or Sell for my portfolio?"

---

## 🤝 The Overlap
Both fields use:
- **DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)**: Predicting future money and bringing it to today's value.
- **Multiples**: Comparing a company to its peers.

**The Difference:** 
A Banker cares about the **Transaction Value**.
A Researcher cares about the **Market Value**.

---

## Which path are you on?
- M&A, IPOs, LBOs $\rightarrow$ **Investment Banking**
- Stock picking, market analysis $\rightarrow$ **Equities**
