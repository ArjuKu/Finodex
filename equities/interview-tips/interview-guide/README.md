# Equities Interview Guide

## Behavioral & Fit Questions

---

## 1. "Pitch Me a Stock"

This is THE most important question for equity research. You MUST prepare.

### Structure:
1. **Thesis** (1 sentence) - "I'm bullish/bearish on [stock] because..."
2. **Business Description** - What does the company do?
3. **Investment Highlights** - Why will it grow?
4. **Risks** - What could go wrong?
5. **Valuation** - DCF, comps, price target
6. **Recommendation** - Buy/Sell/Hold

### Example Template:
> "I'm recommending a BUY on [Company]. [Company] is a [industry] leader that [key value proposition]. Our base case implies [X]% upside to [$Y] target price. The key catalysts are [list]. Risks include [list], but I believe these are priced in at current levels."

---

## 2. "Walk Me Through Your Resume"

Same formula as banking, but emphasize:
- **Analytical skills** - Excel, modeling, data analysis
- **Market interest** - Do you read WSJ, Bloomberg, etc.?
- **Why equities** - Not just "I like stocks"

---

## 3. "Why Equity Research?"

### Good Answers:
- "I enjoy combining analytical skills with writing - I want to be the expert who clients call for insights on [sector]"
- "I want to build deep expertise in a sector and be the 'go-to' person"
- "I want to work in an environment where ideas are debated and I'm incentivized to be right"

### Bad Answers:
- ❌ "IB hours are too bad"
- ❌ "I just want to trade"

---

## 4. "What's Your Investment Style?"

| Style | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Growth** | Focus on companies with high growth |
| **Value** | Focus on undervalued companies |
| **GARP** | Growth at reasonable price |
| **Quantitative** | Data-driven, systematic |

Be honest about your style and back it up with examples.

---

## 5. "What Sector Do You Want to Cover?"

### Tips:
1. **Pick one** - Don't say "I'm open to anything"
2. **Know why** - Show passion and knowledge
3. **Show recent work** - Mention a stock you've researched

### Good Answer:
> "I'm most interested in technology, particularly SaaS. I've been following the space closely and recently analyzed [Company]. I think the shift to cloud creates a multi-year tailwind..."

---

## 6. "What's Your View on [Famous Stock/Topic]?"

### What They're Testing:
- Do you follow markets?
- Can you form a view?
- Can you defend it?

### Framework:
1. **Current situation** - What's happening?
2. **Bull case** - Why it could go up
3. **Bear case** - Why it could go down
4. **Your view** - With reasoning

---

## 7. "How Do You Value a Company?"

### Must Know Methods:
1. **DCF** - Intrinsic value
2. **Comps** - Relative value
3. ** precedent Transactions** - M&A valuation
4. **Dividend Discount** - For dividend payers

---

## 8. "What Happens to [Metric] If [Variable] Changes?"

Common scenarios:
- What happens to net income if revenue grows 10%?
- What happens to WACC if interest rates rise?
- What happens to P/E if growth slows?

**Key:** Show you understand the relationships between financial statements and metrics.
