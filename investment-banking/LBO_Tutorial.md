## 1. The "House Mortgage" Metaphor
Imagine buying a house for **$1,000,000**.
- You use only **$100,000** of your own money (Equity).
- You take a **$900,000** mortgage from a bank (Debt).
- You rent out the house and use that rent to pay down the mortgage.
- After 5 years, you sell the house for **$1,200,000**.
- You pay off the $600,000 debt and walk away with **$600,000**.

**The Result**: You turned $100k into $600k (6x return!) because you used a "mortgage" to buy the business.

---

## 2. The Private Equity Dictionary
- **Leverage**: Using debt to buy a company.
- **Paydown**: Using the target company's cash to pay off the debt.
- **IRR**: The annual return on the buyer's equity investment.
- **MoM (Multiple of Money)**: How many times your original money you got back.

---

## 3. The LBO Process
1. **Sources & Uses**: Where is the money coming from and where is it going?
2. **Paydown**: For 5 years, use all profits to pay off debt.
3. **Exit**: Sell the company. Because debt is smaller, the equity is much bigger!

## 4. Nuance: IRR
The most important metric in an LBO is **IRR**. It's the "interest rate" your investment earns.
- **Time Matters**: Turning $1M into $2M in 3 years is better than 10 years!

**Real Case: RJR Nabisco (1988)** - The famous KKR buyout chronicled in *Barbarians at the Gate*.

---

## 5. Summary
LBOs are about **financial engineering**.
1. Buy with debt (**Sources**).
2. Pay off debt using the company's cash (**Paydown**).
3. Sell the debt-free company for a massive profit (**Exit**).
