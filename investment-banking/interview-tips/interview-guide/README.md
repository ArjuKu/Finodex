# Banking Interview Guide

## Behavioral & Fit Questions

---

## 1. "Walk Me Through Your Resume"

### The Formula
1. **Opening** - One sentence on who you are
2. **Academic background** - Degree, relevant coursework
3. **Previous roles** - Brief, highlight finance-related experiences
4. **Why banking** - Connect your story to the role
5. **Why this firm** - Specific to the bank

### Example Answer
> "I studied Finance at [University] where I focused on corporate valuation and took courses in financial modeling. During my internship at [Company], I built a DCF model to value a potential acquisition target. This experience solidified my interest in investment banking, specifically [sector/product area]. I'm excited about [Bank Name] because..."

---

## 2. "Why Investment Banking?"

### What Banks Look For
- **Technical skills** - You understand the work
- **Work ethic** - You know it's demanding
- **Client-facing** - You enjoy deal work
- **Learning curve** - You want to build skills fast

### Good Answer Framework
1. **Deal exposure** - What attracted you
2. **Skill development** - What you'll learn
3. **Exit opportunities** - (If asked) Where it leads

### What NOT to Say
❌ "I want to work long hours for low pay"
❌ "I want to do IB because of the exit opportunities"
❌ "I couldn't do engineering/consulting"

---

## 3. "Why [Bank Name]?"

### Research Checklist
- [ ] Recent deals they've worked on
- [ ] Industry coverage groups
- [ ] Culture (MD interviews, Glassdoor)
- [ ] Office location
- [ ] Any personal connections

### Example
> "I'm interested in [Bank] because of your leading position in [sector]. I saw you advised on [recent deal], which demonstrated your [specific capability]. Additionally, I connected with [person/team] who highlighted the collaborative culture here."

---

## 4. "Tell Me About a Time You Worked in a Team"

### STAR Method
- **Situation** - Set the context
- **Task** - Your responsibility
- **Action** - What YOU did specifically
- **Result** - Outcome (with numbers if possible)

### Example
> "In my senior year, I led a 5-person team for a capstone project where we had to analyze a potential LBO for a mid-market company. **Situation**: We had 8 weeks and conflicting schedules. **Task**: I was responsible for coordinating the team and ensuring we met deadlines. **Action**: I created a shared timeline, assigned sections based on strengths, and held weekly check-ins. **Result**: We received the highest grade in the class and our analysis was so thorough that the professor used it as a teaching example."

---

## 5. "Tell Me About a Time You Failed"

### Key Points
- Be honest (they know everyone fails)
- Show what you learned
- Show growth

### Example
> "During my internship, I was asked to build a comparable company analysis for a pitch book. I spent too much time perfecting the formatting instead of focusing on getting the multiples right. My VP pointed out that the analysis was beautiful but missing key insights. I learned to prioritize content over aesthetics and now always ask 'What's the key takeaway?' before diving into the details."

---

## 6. "What Are Your Strengths?"

### Framework
1. **Strength** - One that matters for banking
2. **Evidence** - Specific example
3. **How you'll use it** - Connect to the job

### Good Strengths for Banking
- Attention to detail
- Ability to work under pressure
- Quick learner
- Client-facing skills
- Leadership

---

## 7. "What Is Your Greatest Weakness?"

### The Strategy
1. **Real weakness** - Not a fake "strength"
2. **What you're doing about it** - Show self-awareness
3. **Progress** - Show improvement

### Example
> "I used to struggle with public speaking. In college, I avoided presentations. I joined Toastmasters last year and have given 5 speeches since. I'm not perfect yet, but I'm much more comfortable presenting to clients, which I know is critical in banking."

---

## 8. "Do You Have Any Questions for Me?"

### Always Ask (2-3)
1. About the role:
   - "What's a typical day/week like for an analyst?"
   - "What do you enjoy most about working here?"

2. About deals:
   - "What's the most interesting deal you're working on?"
   - "How has [recent market event] affected your pipeline?"

3. About culture:
   - "How does the team handle work-life balance?"
   - "What's the training program like?"

### Never Ask
❌ "How much do you make?"
❌ "Is this job easy?"
❌ "Can I take vacation next month?"
